The 13 Cycles: 21 Variations

Each cycle begins on the first and ends on the 13th. Each poem is a different way of living that pattern, thinking through it, surviving it, or surrendering to it. Read them individually or as a spiral returning to the same wound.

About the Collection

On the Title: "13 Ways" refers to 13 cycles of return and departure. Each cycle: 3 days of living together, followed by leaving on the 13th. A pattern repeated 13 times. This collection documents that compulsion—the same wound, revisited 13 different ways.

How to Live in 13 Days and Die in 13 Different Ways is not about a single heartbreak, but a repeating cycle of loss. The 21 poems are variations on the same moment—return and departure—explored from different angles, emotional states, and degrees of clarity.

The collection opens with a triptych (For Me, For God, For You), moves through 17 thematic poems that circle the same pattern obsessively, and closes with a prose essay interrogating whether art can exist independent of the wound that created it.

This is not a narrative of recovery. It is a document of compulsion—what happens when you return 13 times to the same date, the same goodbye, the same 13th.
