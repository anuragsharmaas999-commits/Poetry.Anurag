About the Collection

How to Live in 13 Days and Die in 13 Different Ways is a collection of 21 poems exploring themes of grief, longing, and survival. Built as a reverse-chronological arc, the work traces a journey from despair toward resilience, capturing the complexity of human emotion through precise, raw language.

Each poem exists as a standalone meditation while contributing to a larger narrative. The collection concludes with a prose essay examining the relationship between the artist and their art—a philosophical inquiry into how we create meaning in the face of loss.
